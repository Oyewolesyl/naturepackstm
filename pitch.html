The Packaging Problem We Can No Longer Ignore

Plastic pollution and agricultural waste — two problems, one solution.

Plastic packaging has become an essential part of modern commerce, but its environmental cost is becoming increasingly difficult to ignore. Millions of plastic bags and packaging materials are discarded every year, contributing to pollution in our communities, waterways, and ecosystems.

01

Plastic Pollution

Millions of plastic bags and packaging materials are discarded every year, contributing to pollution in our communities, waterways, and ecosystems across Nigeria.

02

Wasted Agricultural Resource

Large quantities of banana stems are discarded after harvest despite containing valuable natural fibres that can be transformed into useful products. A resource treated as waste.

03

An Untapped Opportunity

This challenge presents an opportunity to rethink how packaging materials are produced and used — and to turn two problems into one solution.

Why This Matters to Us

Waste should not be the end of a product's story.

At Looped Innovation, our motivation comes from seeing two problems existing side by side: growing plastic pollution and the underutilisation of agricultural resources. Rather than viewing banana stems as waste, we see them as a valuable raw material capable of supporting more sustainable manufacturing practices.

We are driven by the belief that innovation should create both environmental and economic value, benefiting businesses, communities, and future generations.

Introducing NaturePacks™

Banana stem waste, transformed into durable packaging.

NaturePacks™ is a sustainable packaging solution developed by Looped Innovation that transforms discarded banana stems into durable paper suitable for packaging applications — using a structured production process involving fibre extraction, pulping, sheet formation, drying, and finishing.
